## Energy Infrastructure Planning under Uncertainty

Capacity expansion models (CEMs) play an important role in decision-making for energy infrastructure planning, determining the optimal size, location, and timing of generation and transmission assets. These models provide systematic tools to assess how factors such as technological innovation, climate events, policy, and socioeconomic considerations shape investment decisions — all inherently made under uncertainty.

The push for decarbonization has fundamentally shifted the uncertainty landscape faced by planners. The mass adoption of variable renewable energy, increased electrification, evolving demand profiles, growing interdependence between energy sectors, and more frequent weather-induced disruptions has introduced new layers of complexity into investment decisions.

This workshop calls for a reassessment of deterministic approaches and, more broadly, whether richer representations of uncertainty are needed for planning in modern power systems. The role that optimization under uncertainty paradigms — stochastic programming, robust optimization, and distributionally robust optimization — can and should play in real-world planning remains an open question.

## Objectives

- Bring together researchers and practitioners to discuss the role of uncertainty in CEMs.
- Identify methodological, computational, and practical gaps in current planning approaches.
- Provide opportunities for PhD students and early-career researchers to contribute to these discussions in an inclusive environment.
